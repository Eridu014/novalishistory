Judaism

Judaism is the religion of the Jewish people dating back to the ancient Kingdom of Israel as written in the Hebrew Bible. The Hebrew Bible presents a monotheist understanding of God, one revealed to the Jewish people through the Prophets, and chronicles the Jewish people as bound to a holy covenant with the demands of a monotheist God.

Zoroastrianism

Zoroastrianism is the religion of the ancient Iranians expressed through the teachings of Zoroaster (Zarathustra) in the Avesta, an ancient collection of texts and rites. Zoroaster taught a dualistic conception of divinity, one good (Ahura Mazda) and one evil (Angra Mainyu) that cosmically battle until evil is eventually vanquished and good triumphs in the Universe.

Islam

Islam is the belief in monotheism as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad by the Angel Gabriel. This message, which was orally transmitted by the Prophet in the early 7th Century CE and codified into the Quran in the 640s CE by Caliph Uthman. The Quran claims to be the last Word of God in the tradition of earlier Prophetic works and will be so until the resurrection of the dead on the Last Day.

Christianity

Christianity is the belief in the divinity, teachings, and resurrection of Jesus of Nazareth as the universal Messiah of humanity who will redeem the world. His divinity is extolled through the four synoptic gospels (Mark, Matthew Luke, and John) written during the late 1st century CE. The religion spread mainly through Greek men of letters and became the official religion of the Roman Empire in 323 CE.

Buddhism

Buddhism is a religion centered upon the teachings of Siddhārtha Gautama, an Indian aristocrat who taught in the 6th-5th Century BCE about the nature of suffering (Duḥkha) and how to overcome such a state to achieve Nirvāṇa - salvation from endless reincarnation.
