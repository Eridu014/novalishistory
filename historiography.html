Islamic Understandings of History

Medieval Muslim historians formed the backbone to Islamic theology. The writers of the hadith tradition were primarily concerned with Islamic history and reconstructing the life of the Prophet. Muslim historians have the honor of being the first historians to be concerned with a universal understanding of history and theories regarding why civilizations rise and fall.

Enlightenment Historiography

The revolution in ideals sparked by the Enlightenment also brought about radically new interpretations of history. Thinkers such as Voltaire, Hume, and Gibbon brought about a new kind of historiography - one that centered history as a purely human affair which should be conducted as objectively as possible using primary resources, evidence, dates and abandoning the prejudices of religious dogmatism.

Hegelian Historiography

Hegel put forth the notion of a retroactively understood Weltgeist that works through history bringing about a more rational order upon the Earth - known as the Cunning of Reason. He placed emphasis on understanding philosophy as the history of philosophy which ultimately ended in his understanding of Absolute Knowledge

Marxist Historiography

Marx revolutionized the study of history with his emphasis on studying the material basis of societies to understand the epiphenomenon of the ideology of societies. Marx's critique of capitalism and bourgouis society led him to theorize the destruction of capitalist society by a class-conscious international proletarian revolution. Marx's theories profoundly influenced the history of the 19th and 20th centuries.
