The Byzantine Empire

The Byzantine Empire was coined by historians to refer to the Eastern half of the Roman Empire after the gradual fall of the West in the 5th and 6th centuries CE. The Byzantine Empire was administered from Constantinople and reached its zenith under Emperor Justinian. The Roman Empire finally fell in 1453 to Ottoman Turkish forces.

Rise of Islam

The religion of Islam was founded in the early 7th century by the Prophet Muhammad. Following the Prophet's death, the Caliphate rapidly expanded and absorbed the former Persian Empire simultaneously conquering Roman provinces until the Umayyad Caliphate reached from al-Andalusia in the west to Khorosan in the east around the year 750 CE.

The Renaissance

The Renaissance is a tradional marker for the end of the Middle Ages. The movement refers to a rebirth in art, culture, and religion grounded in a humanist understanding of the world that enveloped the republican city-states dotted in and around the Italian peninsula in the 15th century CE.
