Modern History
The Reformation
1517 - 1648

The Reformation was a religous reform movement initiated by the actions of Martin Luther, a German religious dissident. The schism within European Christianity was a significant factor in the civil strife and warfare that rampaged throughout Europe in the 16th and 17th centuries.

Colonization of the New World
1491 - 1791

Despite previous enchroaches into the New World, Christopher Columbus' voyages to the New World in 1492 initated the globe-shattering process of European colonization of the Americas and the Columbian exchange that marked a pivotal event in the beginning of the modern world.

The Scientific Revolution
1543-1687

Tremendous philosophical, religous, and intellectual changes in the 15th - 17th centuries gave rise to our modern understanding of scientific theory and observation.

The Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution refers to the short period of drastic economic and social changes initiated by innovations in mechanics that led to the rise of mass production, industrial labor, and the explosion of worldwide global trade.

The Enlightenment

The Enlightenment is a general term referring to the various currents of intellectual, moral, and political trends in the 18th century.
